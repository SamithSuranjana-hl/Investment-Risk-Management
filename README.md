# Investment & Risk Management Project (Coursera)

This project explores the risk and performance characteristics of major FAANG stocks and the S&P 500 using practical investment analysis techniques. It was completed as part of the Investment and Risk Management course on Coursera, with all calculations performed in Excel.

## Project Overview

The goal of the project was to evaluate each asset’s risk profile, performance, and sensitivity to market movements. The analysis covers:

Monthly return calculations

Volatility measurement (standard deviation)

Beta estimation against the S&P 500

Treynor Ratio for risk-adjusted performance

Value at Risk (VaR) at 95% and 99%

Portfolio-level risk aggregation

What’s Included

Excel workbook containing all calculations

Charts visualizing VaR levels and Treynor Ratio

Summary tables comparing risk metrics across FAANG stocks and SPX

Final output used in the Coursera assessment

Key Insights:
Comparison of systematic risk (beta) across FAANG components

Identification of assets with stronger risk-adjusted returns

Evaluation of downside risk under different confidence intervals

How individual assets contribute to total portfolio risk

Tools Used : Microsoft Excel

Financial functions and custom formulas

Market data provided within the course

Purpose:
This project reflects my workflow for analyzing market behavior, evaluating risk, and interpreting performance metrics across equities. It forms part of my ongoing development in trading, market research, and quantitative analysis.
